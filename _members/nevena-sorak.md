Nevena is a student studying a Bachelor of Psychology (Honours) at Adelaide University. She is currently completing her honours year, undertaking a project exploring theoretical models underpinning the representations and limitations of visual working memory, supervised by Dr William Ngiam at the Perception, Attention, Learning and Memory (PALM) Lab.

Nevena is interested in investigating observable behaviour and cognitive task performance, and how these relate to neural processes. She is also interested in the broader implications of mechanisms such as neuroplasticity for understanding human behaviour, and hopes to continue her studies with a PhD in the field.
